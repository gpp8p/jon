A Road Trip Through the American West...
Jon Hunner has “enjoyed a varied career. He performed as a street clown (including walking and juggling seventy miles from Santa Fe to Albuquerque), traded commodities at the Chicago Board of Trade, earned a degree in philosophy from St. John’s College, and operated an art services corporation in Santa Fe. He then earned a doctorate in history from the University of New Mexico and taught for over twenty years at New Mexico State University. He specializes in 20th century U.S. History and Public History. He published two award-winning books – Inventing Los Alamos and J. Robert Oppenheimer, the Cold War, and the Atomic West – and wrote numerous chapters and articles on a variety of topics including the history of the West, nuclear history, oral history, historic preservation, and living history. Over the years, he has worked with various communities in the United States as well as in Europe to preserve their heritages through restoring historic buildings, interviewing elders, and conducting living history events with students of all ages. On a sabbatical in 2016, he embarked on an extended road trip driving 65,000 miles around the country to places where history actually happened. He visited 150 units of the National Park Service and 250 history museums and historic sites. From these travels, Hunner wrote Driven by The Past: A Road Trip Through the American West which is a place-based narrative of the history of the region. He currently lives in northern France where he and his wife are rehabilitating her family’s historic home where a young Mozart spent the night in 1763.
